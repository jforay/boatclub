What to expect

A simple, proven flow — overview, practice, check-in, and a clear next step.

  1. 1

    Pre-training brief

    Goals for the day, safety basics, local rules, and how club outings work.

    • Reservation + arrival timing
    • Safety gear + required checks
    • Weather and “go / no-go” decisions
  2. 2

    On-water practice

    Hands-on boat control with coaching — slow-speed handling first, then real scenarios.

    • Leaving/approaching the dock
    • Throttle control, turns, trim basics
    • Anchoring, idle zones, navigation awareness
  3. 3

    Skills check

    A low-stress review to confirm you’re ready — and identify what you want more practice on.

    • Docking + basic maneuvering
    • Safety procedures
    • Returning and securing the boat
  4. 4

    Wrap-up + next steps

    We answer questions, recommend next outings, and outline any optional refresher or specialty sessions.

FAQ

Quick answers for new members.

Do I need boating experience?
No — training is built for beginners and tailored to your comfort level.
Can my spouse / co-member train too?
Yes. We recommend it so everyone feels confident operating and assisting.
How long does training take?
Typically 3–4 hours, depending on experience and the skills you want to practice.
Can I get a refresher later?
Absolutely — refreshers and specialty sessions are available as needed.
